USYC Treasury Yield

The agent's $23,600 USDC treasury is parked in USYC (US Yield Coin by Hashnote) on Arc - a T-bill-backed ERC-4626 vault earning ~4% APY. Every cycle, treasury.rebalance() checks if the operational USDC float is low. If it is, it redeems USYC shares back to USDC to fund the next round of payments.

~$944/yr yield covers full Year 3+ maintenance budget exactly.
USYC: 0xe9185...da86C on Arc Testnet

x402 Nanopayments

Every ecological data request is a micropayment. The agent calls GatewayClient.pay(url) - the HTTP response IS the purchased data. No approval, no invoice, no wallet popup. The API price lives in the 402 header; the agent pays or moves on.

$0.001 per ecology risk score
$0.001 per verification call

ERC-8183 Job Protocol

When two models agree a risk is real, the agent opens an on-chain job contract. USDC flows into escrow at fund(), releases to the provider at complete(). Full 6-state machine, fully autonomous. In CONSERVATION mode the agent skips jobs to protect the treasury.

Ownership Breakdown

Detroit Land Bank Authority (DLBA) holds 59,617 lots - down from 67,000 in 2016 as the city slowly works through its backlog. Private owners hold 41,685 more. Another 5,381 sit with the city's Planning and Development Department.

Mowing cost: $13.44/lot x 5 cuts per year = $6.72M annually. Since 2014 the city has spent $456M demolishing 27,000 structures. Only 942 remain. The demolition phase is ending. The question is what comes next.

The Opportunity

Chadsey-Condon is on Detroit's southwest side (48216), a working-class community built around the industrial base. Like many neighborhoods, heavily impacted by depopulation. But deep roots remain - neighbors tend gardens, watch out for each other.

Dryad's V2 governance model airdrops stewardship NFTs to neighbors within 500 feet of each lot. The land's future belongs to the people who actually live near it - not to investors or distant institutions.

The DLBA's CREATE-A-PROJECT program: $250/lot for LLCs or 501(c)3 organizations. Contact LBP@detroitmi.gov. The agent handles ongoing maintenance compliance autonomously - no landlord needed, just smart contracts and sensors.

Native Ecology - What We're Restoring

Detroit sits on glacial lakeplain - some of the rarest ecological communities in North America. Less than 1% of the original lakeplain prairie survives. Dryad's 9 lots are a chance to restore a fragment of what once stretched for miles.

Lakeplain Oak Openings (G2/S1)

Globally imperiled. Bur oak, swamp white oak, big bluestem, Indian grass, wild bergamot. These communities evolved with periodic fire - the same open-canopy structure that made Detroit's lakeshore habitat for species now rare or absent.

Dryad's planting plan: 300 saplings from Cold Stream Farm. 80 Bur Oak, 60 Swamp White Oak, 40 White Oak, 30 Pin Oak. Prairie Moon seed mix - 25+ species, 60% grasses / 40% forbs, full-season bloom April through October.

Species of Concern

Kirtland's Snake (state threatened) has been documented on Detroit vacant lots. Monarch butterfly (federal candidate) - every milkweed plant counts. Rusty-patched bumble bee (federally endangered) - historically present in this corridor. Eastern Meadowlark and Bobolink nest in urban grasslands when they're tall enough.

The Detroit River flyway brings warblers through during migration. Even 9 lots of native habitat adds a stepping stone to the migratory corridor.

Invasive Species - 3-Tier Priority System

Tier 1 - Remove

Common/glossy buckthorn, autumn olive, Amur honeysuckle, multiflora rose, Oriental bittersweet. Contractor removal, MNFI-guided protocol.

Tier 2 - Manage

Non-native Phragmites, reed canary grass, purple loosestrife, spotted knapweed, garlic mustard, Japanese knotweed. Annual monitoring and targeted treatment.

Tier 3 - Assess

Tree of Heaven (300K seeds/year, ailanthone allelopathic toxin). Critical note: Native Phragmites (subsp. americanus) has reddish stems - leave it alone.

The Spotted Lanternfly threat: Arriving from Asia via international shipping, this planthopper devastates hardwood trees and vineyards. Once established, it spreads by hitchhiking on vehicles and shipping containers. Dryad's iNaturalist monitoring flags any observations immediately for rapid response.

iNaturalist Project

All observations feed Dryad's ecological health score, which feeds the agent's risk cycle. Community scientists photograph any living thing on or near the lots - the AI + community ID the species. This is the sensor layer of the autonomous stewardship system.

Restoration Plan

Annual Operating Cost

Years 1-2: ~$1,445/yr (active invasive removal). Year 3+: ~$945/yr (established, maintenance only). Per-lot by year 3: ~$105/yr. City mowing baseline: $67-170/lot/yr. The agent is cheaper and leaves habitat instead of lawn.

Treasury and Operating Model

Arc-Native Yield: USYC

The $23,600 USDC treasury is deposited into USYC on Arc - Hashnote's US Yield Coin, backed by short-duration US Treasuries. USYC is an ERC-4626 vault: deposit USDC, receive shares that appreciate as T-bill interest accrues. Redeem shares back to USDC whenever the operational wallet runs low.

At ~4% APY: $23,600 × 0.04 = $944/yr. The Year 3+ maintenance budget is exactly $945/yr. The forest pays for itself from T-bill yield, perpetually, with no external revenue needed.

Also live on Arc testnet: Aave V3 (3-6%), Morpho (4-7%), Superform (yield aggregator). Dryad-Arc uses USYC as the primary source for its simplicity and direct Circle integration.

Spending Autonomy

Three spending modes govern all autonomous payments, determined by treasury health at each cycle start:

NORMAL: Full operations. Property taxes from yield, x402 ecology data ($0.001/call), ERC-8183 restoration jobs ($0.10/event), iNaturalist monitoring.

CONSERVATION: x402 data purchases continue (cheap), ERC-8183 jobs suspended to protect treasury. Triggered when USDC float < $5 and USYC < $10 total.

CRITICAL: All non-essential spending suspended. Human governance required. Triggered when total treasury < $2 USDC.

Designed to run 50+ years on the $23.6K treasury at current T-bill rates.

Community Governance

Dryad is built as an agent but designed as a community institution. The V2 governance model makes the lots' nearest neighbors its primary stewards - not token holders, not investors, the people who actually live there.

Proximity Airdrop

Residents within 500 feet of any Dryad lot get a free stewardship NFT. Mirrors the DLBA Side Lot program which already prioritizes adjacent homeowners. The agent delivers the airdrop automatically based on on-chain address verification.

Participation Airdrop

10+ verified iNaturalist observations earns 1 NFT. Volunteer work day earns 1 NFT. Complete a paid contractor job earns 1 NFT. Block club endorsement earns NFTs for all members. Proof-of-stewardship, not proof-of-capital.

Soulbound by Default

Stewardship NFTs are non-transferable unless the DAO votes to unlock. Prevents speculation and absentee ownership. If someone moves, the NFT can be reclaimed by DAO vote and re-airdropped to the new resident.

Equal Governance

1 NFT = 1 vote. No whale dynamics. Simple majority for routine maintenance, 75% supermajority for land use changes. The agent executes decisions. The community decides. The land heals.

Why this matters: Most crypto land projects sell tokens to fund acquisition. Dryad gives them away to the people who actually live there. This is not DeFi extracting value from Detroit - it's DeFi returning ownership to Detroit's communities.

ecology-api

Express server with Circle Gateway seller middleware. Listens for x402 payment headers, uses EIP-3009 TransferWithAuthorization for Circle settlement. Returns wildfire/ecological risk scores from simulated sensor data. Port 3001.

verification-api

Second x402-gated service. Takes an ecology score from the primary model and returns an independent verification. If both models agree the risk is high, the agent proceeds to open an ERC-8183 job. Prevents false positives from triggering costly on-chain actions. Port 3002.

agent (risk cycle)

Core operating loop. Every 60 seconds: observe coordinates, buy ecology score via x402, optionally buy verification, open ERC-8183 job if both models agree, record ERC-8004 reputation event after completion. Express API for dashboard. Port 3010.
