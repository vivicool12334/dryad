# The Economics of Blight - Costs, Multipliers & ROI

*Compiled April 2026. Investor-grade citations. Detroit-weighted.*

## The Full Cost of Blight (Beyond Mowing)

### Detroit
- **$6.72M/year** on mowing alone ($13.44/lot × 5 cuts × 100K lots)
- **$456M spent demolishing 27,000 homes** since 2014 (Hardest Hit Fund + city funds)
- **$300M+ present value health burden** from arson-related CO emissions from vacant structures (Devil's Night study)
- Detroit Fire Department responded to **9,000+ structural fires in 2011; 85% in vacant homes**
- Source: "Hidden Costs of Blight and Arson in Detroit: Evidence From a Natural Experiment in Devil's Night," *Ecological Economics*, Vol. 157 (2019), pp. 266-277

### Baltimore (Comprehensive Model)
- **$100.6 million annually** across three categories:
  - Identified budget line items: $21M
  - 911 calls for vacant blocks: $29.6M
  - Capital budget for stabilization/demolition: $50M
- Cost per vacant property: **$1,472-1,979/year** in police/fire response alone
- Source: The Abell Foundation, "The Costs of Baltimore's Vacant Housing," 2022

### Toledo, Ohio
- **$3.8 million/year** total municipal drain from vacancy:
  - Code enforcement: $1.0M
  - Nuisance abatement: $800K
  - Police dispatch: $600K
  - Fire dispatch: $1.4M
- Source: Center for Community Progress, "The Costs of Vacant, Abandoned, and Deteriorated Properties," 2022

### Austin, Texas (Crime Multiplier)
- Blocks with unsecured vacant buildings experience:
  - **3.2x more drug calls** to police
  - **1.8x more theft calls**
  - **2x more violent crime calls**
- Source: Center for Community Progress

## Tax Revenue Loss

### Philadelphia Model
- **$3.6 billion in lost property values** citywide from vacancy
- Translates to **$2M+ annually** in lost property tax revenue
- **$20M+ annually** in direct city maintenance costs
- 19,000+ vacant parcels - Detroit has 5-6x more
- Source: ECONSULT Corporation, "Vacant Land Management in Philadelphia," 2010

### Detroit Extrapolation
- 100,000+ vacant lots = estimated **$5-10M+ annual direct tax loss** (based on Philadelphia/Atlanta comparables)
- Additional: ~35% of vacant property owners delinquent on taxes (Baltimore benchmark) = compounding revenue loss

## The Tipping Point

### Critical Vacancy Threshold
- **4% vacancy rate = the tipping point** beyond which neighborhood decline becomes rapid
- Below 4%: individual vacant properties don't materially change neighborhood
- At/above 4%: "Things go south very quickly" - rapid property value decline, population loss, tax base erosion
- Detroit's estimated vacancy: 20-25% - **far above tipping point**
- Source: Johns Hopkins Hub Magazine, "Putting a Price on Baltimore's Vacant Housing," Winter 2022; researcher Thomas Miller

## Rust Belt ROI Benchmarks

### Youngstown/Mahoning County (10-Year Study)
- $21M invested in blight removal (2009-2019)
- **$33.2M in economic benefits returned**
- ROI: **$1.50 per $1.00 spent on demolition**
- **$1.43 per $1.00 spent on greening/vacant lot improvements**
- Study understates impact by excluding property taxes collected from restored properties
- Source: Youngstown State University, commissioned by Mahoning County Land Bank

### Cleveland
- 8 Ohio cities (including Cleveland) spent **$15M annually** on code enforcement, building boarding, demolitions, maintenance, police/fire response to vacancy
- Source: Center for Community Progress; Reimagining Cleveland Vacant Lot Greening Program

### Philadelphia Property Value Impact
- Properties within 150 feet of vacant/abandoned property: **-$7,627 net value loss**
- Properties within 500 feet: **-9.4% value loss**
- Source: Econsult Corporation; Lincoln Institute of Land Policy

## Property Value Gains from Greening

### Philadelphia Greening Program
- Houses within 1,000 feet of greened vacant lot: **+4% property value increase**
- Tree planting on vacant lots: **+10% property value** (aggregate $4M gain from program)
- Source: Garvin et al.; Kimbauer et al.

### National Urban Forestry Economic Impact
- Direct economic footprint of private sector urban forestry: **$34.3B (2017)**
- Direct employment: **323,000 jobs**
- Employee compensation: **$15.2B in wages/benefits**
- Every **$1 invested in street trees returns $3 in benefits**
- Source: Arbor Day Foundation, "Economic Footprint and Quality-of-Life Benefits of Trees" (2017 IMPLAN data)

### Retail Impact
- Green landscapes in business districts attract shoppers who spend **9-12% more**
- Shoppers give **30% higher quality ratings** to products in tree-heavy retail districts
- Source: University of Washington; DeepRoot/Trust for Public Land

## Compounding Cost of Abandonment

### The Time Escalation
- Typical abandoned building remediation: **$2,550** (one-time)
- Typical vacant lot remediation: **$1,597** (one-time)
- Annual maintenance thereafter: **~$180/year**
- "Longer a property remains abandoned, the higher the cost of renovation" - cost increases exponentially with years of vacancy
- Properties where deterioration exceeds cost-effective repair = permanent loss of redevelopment potential
- Source: Branas et al., "Urban Blight Remediation as a Cost-Beneficial Solution to Firearm Violence," *AJPH* 106(12): 2158-64 (2016)

### Cumulative Service Costs
- Each additional year a property sits vacant = cumulative police/fire costs + property value loss
- Even after property is sold, neighboring properties experience $25,000+ value loss for ≥2 years
- Source: Treasury Department analysis (Klein, 2017)
