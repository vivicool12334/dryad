# Green Space Benefits - Research & Data

## Health & Mental Health
- 63% reduction in poor mental health near greened vacant lots (Philadelphia study, cited in DFC "Saving for the Future")
- 76% increase in outdoor recreation near greened lots
- Green space exposure reduces stress, improves mental health, promotes physical activity, lowers cardiovascular disease risk
- Park users meeting 150 min/week CDC recommendation avoid $8M+/year in healthcare costs (Baton Rouge parish study)
- Structural barriers restricting green space access are a significant factor in disproportionately high cardiovascular disease in African American communities

## Crime & Safety
- 40% reduction in violent crime near greened vacant lots (University of Michigan study, Flint, MI)
- Community-led greening outperforms municipal-only approaches by 2× in crime reduction
- Areas receiving no care saw a slight increase in violent crime - the counterfactual matters
- Greened lots signal community care and active stewardship, changing social dynamics
- Reductions documented in felony assaults, aggravated assaults, and homicides

## Property Values & Economics
- 8–20% property value increase near green spaces, effect reaches up to half a mile
- Street trees and yard trees add 3–15% to home values across neighborhoods
- In high-vacancy neighborhoods (>3% vacant land share), greening lots recovers up to 19% of surrounding home values
- Every $1 invested in street trees returns $3 in benefits
- Green landscapes in business districts attract shoppers who spend 9–12% more
- Homes within 500 ft of parks sell for 5% more

## Heat Island & Energy
- Tree canopy lowers pedestrian temperatures by up to 21°F
- Increasing tree cover by 10% reduces air temperatures by up to 2°C
- Street trees in Grand Rapids, Michigan saved $722,000/year in energy costs
- Detroit: 86% of neighborhoods run 8°F+ hotter than rural areas
- Approximately 3,160 Detroit residents live in intra-urban heat islands 10°F+ hotter than nearby areas
- Suburban neighbors have 30% more tree canopy - a legacy of white flight

## Stormwater & Water Quality
- Urban trees across 242 U.S. counties avoid ~110 billion gallons of stormwater runoff annually
- Philadelphia green infrastructure NPV: $1.94–4.45 billion over 40 years (tree planting, permeable surfaces, green roofs)
- Tree canopy slows rainfall; roots and soil filter/absorb water, reducing volume that reaches combined sewer systems
- Detroit aims to manage 48 million gallons through green infrastructure

## Carbon & Air Quality
- A single mature tree sequesters up to 48 lbs of CO₂/year
- U.S. urban trees collectively store 700 million tonnes of carbon ($14.3 billion value)
- U.S. urban trees sequester 22.8 million tonnes of carbon/year ($460 million/year)
- Urban trees remove 711,000 metric tons of air pollution annually ($3.8 billion value)
- Green infrastructure can deliver $9.1 million/year in healthcare savings from improved air quality alone

## Biodiversity & Ecological Services
- Movement between connected habitat patches is 68% greater than between unconnected patches (across small mammals, butterflies, plants, pollinators)
- Vegetated habitat area is the single strongest predictor of urban biodiversity (five-continent analysis)
- Converting urban land to park/green space is one of the most effective interventions for species richness
- 87 leading global food crops rely on pollinators (35% of production volume)
- Global welfare loss from pollinator decline projected at $729 billion/year (0.9% of GDP)
- Green corridors facilitate gene dispersal, migration, and expanded habitat range
- Each lot is a node in the corridor: more native plants → more pollinators → better pollination → more native reproduction → expanding habitat

## Green Space Equity - Detroit Context
- Only 6% of Detroit's land is dedicated to parks/recreation vs. 15% national median
- 87% of Detroit buildings are more than a quarter-mile walk from a park
- Higher social vulnerability scores correlate directly with lower green space availability
- Detroit chief parks planner goal: 95%+ of population within a 10-minute walk of a park
- Tree canopy declined 23% since 1950; 286 of 875 block groups below 75 on Tree Equity Score
