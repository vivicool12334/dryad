# The Human Cost of Urban Blight - Research & Data

*Compiled April 2026. Investor-grade citations. Detroit-weighted.*

## The Gold Standard: Gun Violence RCTs

### Branas et al. 2018 - Philadelphia Citywide Cluster RCT
- **29% reduction in gun violence** across all neighborhoods
- **58% reduction in fear of going outside** due to safety concerns
- **22% decrease in burglaries**
- **30% drop in total crime**
- In neighborhoods below poverty line: 29.1% reduction in gun assaults, 13.3% reduction in all crimes
- 110 clusters containing 541 vacant lots; randomized to greening, trash cleanup only, and no intervention
- Cost: ~$1,600 to transform an abandoned lot; $180/year to maintain
- First citywide RCT of its kind - experimental evidence, not quasi-experimental
- Source: Branas, CC, South, E, Kondo, MC, et al., "Citywide cluster randomized trial to restore blighted vacant land and its effects on violence, crime, and fear," *PNAS*, 2018; 115(12):2946-2951

### Branas et al. 2021 - Abandoned Building Remediation RCT
- **13.12% reduction in gun assaults** post-intervention
- **8.43% reduction in weapons violations**
- Full remediation (windows, doors, cleanup) works; cleanup-only shows no effect
- 258 buildings studied
- Source: Branas et al., *JAMA Internal Medicine*, 2021

### Economic Return
- **$1 invested in vacant lot remediation returns $26 in net benefits** from violence reduction alone
- Less than 5% of remediated lots became developed - no gentrification/displacement effect
- Source: Branas & MacDonald, Penn IUR

## Detroit-Specific Crime & Vacancy

### Vacancy-Homicide Correlation
- Percentage of vacant housing units shows strong positive relationship to homicide rates in Detroit neighborhoods (block-group level spatial analysis)
- Source: MSU Institute for Public Policy and Social Research (IPPSR), "Homicide, Home Vacancies, and Population Change in Detroit"

### Demolition & Crime (Mixed Results)
- Demolitions of vacant buildings in Detroit associated with 16-19% reductions in arson; significant reduction in burglary
- Source: Journal of Criminal Justice, 2019 analysis of Detroit demolitions
- BUT: University of Maryland study (2022) found block-level violent crime in Detroit in 2017 was "nearly identical" to a counterfactual scenario with no demolitions - demolition alone may not be enough
- Source: ScienceDirect, "Effects of building demolitions on firearm violence in Detroit, Michigan," 2022

### Flint Land Bank Model
- **40% reduction in assaults and violent crimes** on street segments with Land Bank-maintained vacant lots
- Examined 7,151 GCLBA-owned vacant properties vs. comparison properties (2015-2018)
- Reductions in youth-involved firearm crime specifically
- Source: Bushman, G. et al., "Associations between land bank ownership and stewardship of vacant properties and crime, violence, and youth victimization in Flint, MI," *American Journal of Community Psychology*, 2023

## Mental Health - Depression & Psychological Impact

### Philadelphia Vacant Lot Greening & Depression (RCT)
- **41.5% reduction in feeling depressed** (p = 0.03)
- **50.9% reduction in feeling worthless** (p = 0.04)
- **62.8% reduction in overall poor mental health** (p = 0.051)
- For residents below poverty line: **68.7% reduction in depression** (p = 0.007)
- Trash cleanup alone showed NO significant mental health benefit - greening was the active ingredient
- Source: South, E, Kondo, MC, Branas, CC et al., "Effect of Greening Vacant Land on Mental Health of Community-Dwelling Adults," *JAMA Network Open*, 2018

## Children: Brain Development, Trauma, Education

### Amygdala Development & Neighborhood Violence
- Living in neighborhoods with high violence alters children's brain development - specifically amygdala reactivity (threat-detection system)
- 708 children and teens ages 7-19 from Michigan Twins Neurogenetic Study
- Protective factor: nurturing/involved parents diminished impact even when exposed
- Source: Suarez, GL & Burt, A (U-M), "Exposure to Community Violence as a Mechanism Linking Neighborhood Disadvantage to Amygdala Reactivity," *APA*, 2024

### School Absenteeism (Detroit)
- Detroit had **47.8% chronic absenteeism rate** (2015-16), highest among major U.S. cities
- Residential vacancy rate, violent crime rate, poverty, and adult asthma rates all correlate with school absenteeism
- Source: University of Michigan / Teachers College Record analysis of 34 major U.S. cities

### Childhood Trauma
- Chronic exposure to urban blight and poverty creates cumulative childhood trauma leading to behavioral, physiological, and developmental problems that persist into adulthood
- Source: National Child Traumatic Stress Network / Family-Informed Trauma Treatment Center, University of Maryland

## Lead Exposure from Demolition (Detroit-Specific)

### Lead Dust from Demolition
- **45 square miles of Detroit (33% of city)** would have faced cumulative lead dust exposure under standard demolition protocols
- **Odds of elevated blood lead increased 35%** with 1 demolition within 400 feet (p < 0.01)
- **Odds increased 59%** with 2+ demolitions within 400 feet (p < 0.01)
- Study: 24,479-25,287 Detroit children tested 2014-2016
- Source: Detroit City Task Force, "Task Force Recommendations for Improving Demolition Safety and Community Health"

### Contaminated Backfill (Ongoing 2025-26)
- Hundreds of Detroit demolition sites backfilled with contaminated dirt containing lead and arsenic above state guidelines
- City testing 500+ sites; commitment to remove/replace contaminated soil
- Source: Detroit Free Press investigations (January 2026)

## Broken Windows & Disorder Signaling

### The Mechanism
- Overgrown vacant lots with trash accumulation are quintessential "broken windows" - they signal abandonment and make a space attractive for illegal activities (gun storage, drug dealing, hiding from police)
- Visible signs of disorder communicate lack of social control, encouraging further disorder and crime
- Source: Lanfear, CC, "Broken Windows, Informal Social Control, and Crime," *Crime & Delinquency*, 2019

### Social Infrastructure (Eric Klinenberg)
- Vacant properties undermine "social infrastructure" - the physical places that enable informal social control and community cohesion
- When neighborhoods are visibly abandoned, residents lose collective efficacy; they withdraw from public space, reducing surveillance and community bonds
- Restoration of vacant land rebuilds social infrastructure by signaling the area is cared for
- Source: Klinenberg, E, *Palaces for the People* (2018)

## Detroit Asthma Crisis
- **16.2% of adults in Detroit had asthma** (2017-2019) vs. 11.1% statewide
- Children in Detroit nearly 2x as likely to have asthma compared to state average
- **Detroit rated "most challenging city in nation to live with asthma"** (AAFA, 2024-2025)
- Asthma hospitalization rates for children: **3x statewide average**
- Source: Michigan Department of Health and Human Services (2021); AAFA rankings

## Resident Testimonials
- "It's Like They're Tearing Down Your Memories" - title of academic study on resident perceptions of vacancy and blight
- Source: ResearchGate, "Resident Perceptions of Property Vacancy and Blight and Its Implications for Community Practice"
- Captures the emotional dimension: blight doesn't just destroy buildings, it destroys historical identity and community memory
