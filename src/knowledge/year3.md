# Year 3 Vision - Dryad 2029 Mockup

A mockup dashboard at dryad.vercel.app/mock.html shows what Dryad looks like in full operation by March 2029.

## Projected Status (Year 3)

- **Lots managed:** 9 (all acquired and restored)
- **Ecosystem health score:** 72% and climbing
- **Species documented:** 47 via iNaturalist (12 native targets established, 3 invasives under active management)
- **Treasury:** Self-sustaining on cross-chain USDC DeFi yield (~4% APY); operating cost ~$945/yr
- **Contractors:** 3 verified, 28 total jobs completed
- **On-chain milestones:** 12 recorded to DryadMilestones.sol

## Key Milestones in the Mockup Timeline

1. **March 2026:** Project launch, first 2 lots acquired via DLBA
2. **June 2026:** Initial invasive clearing, first iNaturalist observations
3. **October 2026:** Fall seed installation across first lots
4. **March 2027:** 5 additional lots acquired, spring planting begins
5. **September 2027:** First prairie bloom season, 25+ species documented
6. **March 2028:** Full 9-lot portfolio established, oak saplings planted
7. **March 2029:** Ecosystem health 72%, treasury self-sustaining, governance NFTs active

## Dashboard Features Shown

- **Overview panel:** Health score, species count, active alerts, treasury balance
- **Species tracker:** Native vs. invasive species with status indicators
- **Decision log:** Autonomous actions taken by the agent with timestamps
- **Contractor table:** Verified contractors, jobs completed, total paid
- **Treasury breakdown:** Holdings, annual costs, yield vs. expenses
- **Timeline:** Visual chronological history with health score progression

## What This Demonstrates

The mockup proves the model works: an autonomous agent can manage land at lower cost than traditional approaches ($105/lot/yr vs. $67-170 for city mowing), while building genuine ecological value that compounds over time. By Year 3, the prairie is self-sustaining and the treasury covers all costs from yield.

This is what "autonomous sustainability" looks like - the AI bootstraps the ecosystem, then the land takes over.
