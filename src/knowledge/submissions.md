# Photo Submissions & Community Observations

Dryad relies on community science to monitor ecological health. There are two main ways to contribute observations.

## iNaturalist (Primary Method)

**Project:** inaturalist.org/projects/dryad-25th-street-parcels-mapping

### How to Submit
1. Download the iNaturalist app (free, iOS & Android)
2. Visit the lots at 25th Street between Ash Street and Beech Street, Detroit
3. Photograph any living thing - plants, animals, fungi, insects
4. Upload with GPS location enabled (the app handles this automatically)
5. The AI + community will identify the species

### What Makes a Good Observation
- Clear, well-lit photo (multiple angles help ID)
- GPS location enabled (observations outside the project boundary won't count)
- One species per observation
- Include habitat context (e.g., "growing in disturbed soil near fence line")

### What Dryad Does With Observations
Every iNaturalist observation feeds into Dryad's ecological health score. The agent uses this data to:
- Track native species establishment progress
- Detect invasive species early (triggers contractor scheduling)
- Monitor seasonal changes and phenology
- Build a biodiversity baseline over time
- Record ecological milestones on-chain
- Mint onchain EAS attestations for research-grade observations (community-verified species ID)

### Onchain Attestations
Research-grade iNaturalist observations - where the community has confirmed the species ID - are automatically attested onchain via the Ethereum Attestation Service (EAS) on Base. Each attestation records the observer, species, GPS location, quality grade, and whether it's an invasive species. This creates a permanent, verifiable biodiversity record. View attestations at base.easscan.org.

## Contractor Proof-of-Work Portal

**URL:** dashboard.dryad.land/Dryad/submit

Approved contractors submit GPS-tagged before/after photos of their work. The flow:
1. Contractor enters their access code (received via email after approval)
2. Uses device camera to capture GPS-tagged photos (minimum 2, maximum 20)
3. Selects work type and writes a description
4. Submits - Dryad's vision model verifies the photos
5. If approved, an onchain EAS attestation is minted on Base with: contractor address, work type, parcel, photo hash, and vision score
6. Contractor is paid in USDC on Base ($50/tx limit, $200/day limit)

Contractors can view their attestations on base.easscan.org - permanent proof of their ecological work.

## Reporting Issues

For reporting issues that don't fit iNaturalist:
- Illegal dumping or debris on the lots
- Fence damage or encroachment
- Standing water or drainage issues
- Vandalism or unauthorized clearing

## Seasonal Volunteer Opportunities

- **April–May:** Spring knotweed and buckthorn removal (most impactful time)
- **June–August:** Monitoring, watering new plantings, invasive spot-checks
- **September–October:** Seed collection from established native plants
- **November–March:** Planning, equipment prep, community outreach

## How Contributions Earn Governance

In V2, active contributors earn soulbound governance NFTs:
- iNaturalist observations within the project boundary
- Verified volunteer hours
- Proximity to the lots (neighbors within 500 ft get free airdrops)

1 NFT = 1 vote in the Snapshot DAO (dryadforest.eth). Non-transferable - no speculation, just stewardship.
