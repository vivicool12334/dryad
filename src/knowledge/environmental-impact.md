# Dryad Environmental Impact — AI Footprint vs. Habitat Benefits

## The Core Argument
Dryad sequesters 64–240x more carbon than its AI operations produce. The habitat pays back Dryad's entire annual carbon footprint in 1–3 days of the growing season. The rest of the year is net removal.

## Dryad's Actual Footprint
Dryad doesn't train models. It makes API calls to existing ones. The marginal cost of one more user on Claude or GPT-4 is negligible — the model and data center exist whether or not Dryad makes a query.

- Energy: 10.8–18 kWh per year (about 9–15 hours of household electricity)
- Carbon (US grid): 4.2–6.9 kg CO2 per year (driving 11–19 miles)
- Carbon (renewable data center): 0.5–0.9 kg CO2 per year
- Water: 40–150 liters per year (one small bathtub)
- Equivalent to about 10–17 minutes of highway driving
- Roughly 1/50th to 1/150th of a single domestic round-trip flight

Sources: Jegham et al. 2025 (TokenPowerBench, arXiv); EIA grid emissions factor 2024; EPA vehicle emissions data

## Carbon Sequestration — What the Habitat Produces
Based on a 22-year study at Cedar Creek (Yang et al., Nature Communications 2019):

- Years 1–13: ~0.45–0.60 metric tons CO2/year on 0.57 acres
- Years 13–22: ~0.60–0.80 metric tons CO2/year (rate accelerates ~32%)
- 22-year total: ~11–13 metric tons CO2
- High-diversity plots (16+ species) stored 178% more carbon than monocultures
- C4 grasses + legumes work together: legumes fix nitrogen, grasses use it to build massive root systems that pump carbon deep into soil
- Deep roots lock carbon into soil for centuries to millennia
- Once established, the habitat is self-sustaining — that's the "autonomous sustainability" in Dryad's thesis

Sources: Yang et al., "Soil carbon sequestration accelerated by restoration of grassland biodiversity," Nature Communications 2019; MN Board of Water and Soil Resources; Scientific Reports 2024

## Ecosystem Benefits (0.57 acres)
- Carbon sequestration: 0.57–0.97 metric tons CO2/year
- Stormwater absorbed: 13,000–25,000 gallons/year
- Temperature reduction: 2–3°C local microclimate cooling
- Pollinator species supported: 30–80 species
- Biodiversity increase: 10–20x vs. abandoned lot
- PM2.5 air pollution removed: 15–50 lbs/year

## The Industry-Wide AI Problem (for honest context)
These are real — Dryad acknowledges them openly:

- Global data centers: 415 TWh in 2024 (1.5% of world electricity), projected to reach 945 TWh by 2030
- US data centers: 183 TWh (4.3% of US electricity), could double by 2030
- Water: 560 billion liters globally in 2024; Google alone used 6 billion gallons
- Two-thirds of new data centers since 2022 are in water-stressed regions
- Northern Virginia wholesale electricity costs up 267% over five years
- Carnegie Mellon projects 8% average nationwide electricity bill increase by 2030 (up to 25% in hotspots)
- Training GPT-4: estimated 51–62 GWh electricity, 1,000–15,000 tons CO2
- Natural gas plant proposals have tripled to power new data centers

Sources: IEA 2024; Pew Research 2025; Google 2024 Environmental Report; WRI 2025; Bloomberg 2025; Carnegie Mellon 2025; Global Energy Monitor 2026

## Key Nuances to Communicate
1. The industry-wide AI energy problem needs systemic solutions — renewable energy mandates, water-free cooling, efficiency improvements, honest accounting
2. The "AI uses water" critique applies to data center operators, not individual applications — criticizing Dryad for data center water usage is like criticizing an electric car owner for coal power plants
3. Not all AI use is equal — running inference for habitat restoration has a different moral calculus than training frontier models for memes
4. AI-assisted management likely has a smaller carbon footprint than purely traditional approaches, but this is an experiment, not a verdict
5. Native habitat restoration alone can't offset the data center industry — a single 100 MW hyperscale data center emits 386,000–463,000 metric tons CO2/year, which would require 227,000–463,000 acres of native prairie to offset (2.5–5x the area of Detroit)

## Detroit Scale-Up Potential
Detroit has 100,000+ vacant lots. If 1% adopted this model (1,000 lots, ~63 acres):
- Carbon sequestration: 90–153 metric tons CO2/year
- Stormwater managed: ~23–44 million gallons/year
- The AI managing this at scale would consume roughly the energy of a few households
