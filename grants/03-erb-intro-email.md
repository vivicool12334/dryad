Hi there,

I'm hoping to connect with whoever manages the Great Lakes Initiative. I built a project called "Dryad" and I'm wondering if it might fit within your current funding priorities.

The short version: Dryad is a theoretical AI agent framework for autonomous land management. I've been building an AI system that can manage native habitat restoration on vacant lots in Detroit. It monitors biodiversity through iNaturalist, coordinates contractors, manages its own treasury through staking yield, and records ecological milestones onchain. The whole thing is designed to be self-sustaining after a one-time setup investment.

I haven't purchased the land yet. I'm currently exploring grant opportunities and figuring out the right fundraising strategy to get this off the ground properly. I'm also in the process of finding a nonprofit to act as my fiscal sponsor for grant applications. The system is built and functional, but I want to make sure the funding and institutional support are in place before acquiring the lots through Detroit's Land Bank.

The ecological angle is what I think connects to your work: we're targeting lakeplain oak openings and wet prairie, the globally imperiled communities that historically covered the Great Lakes lakeplain. Less than 1% of the original range survives. Detroit's 100,000+ vacant lots are an unusual opportunity to bring some of that back, and autonomous technology could make it scalable in a way that volunteer-driven models can't sustain.

I'd love a brief conversation about whether this is the kind of thing you'd consider before I submit through your grants portal. Happy to send more detail or walk through the system.
