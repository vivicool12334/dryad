## 2/7

Detroit has 100,000+ vacant lots across 18 square miles. The city spends $6.72M/year just to mow them.

We're turning that liability into ecological infrastructure.

## 3/7

Dryad runs a 24-hour autonomous loop:

- Checks biodiversity via iNaturalist
- Scores ecosystem health
- Detects invasive species
- Emails contractors for removal
- Pays them in USDC on Base
- Records milestones onchain

No human in the loop for day-to-day operations.

## 4/7

The target: lakeplain oak openings and wet prairie. Globally imperiled ecosystems — less than 1% of the original range survives.

Detroit's vacant lots sit on the ancient bed of glacial Lake Maumee. The right soil for the right restoration.

## 5/7

The financial model: a one-time treasury of $27K in staked ETH generates ~$945/yr in yield.

Year 3+ operating costs: $945/yr. Per lot: ~$105/yr — less than the city pays to mow.

You fund it once. The forest runs forever.

## 6/7

- 21°F urban cooling
- $3 returned for every $1 invested

This changes neighborhoods.
