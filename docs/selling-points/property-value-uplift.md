## One-paragraph version (for the website "Why this matters" section)

A vacant, blighted lot doesn't just sit there. It actively pulls down every neighbor's home value by anywhere from 4 to 20 percent. The strongest causal evidence in the field, a Penn-led randomized controlled trial in Philadelphia, shows what happens when those same lots get cleaned, planted, and maintained: surrounding home values rise 4.3 percent in the first year and 13 percent cumulatively over six years, crime falls 9 percent, and burglary falls 14 percent. A single street tree adds about $12,800 in sale-price value to the home it fronts plus its immediate neighbors. Across nine Dryad-managed parcels in Chadsey-Condon, that arithmetic compounds into hundreds of thousands of dollars of value flowing back to the families who already live next door, with the largest gains landing in the lowest-income blocks.

## Numbers cleared for use (with sourcing in research doc)

These are the specific figures you can put on the website, in slides, or in grant applications without overstating. Each is traceable to a peer-reviewed study or USFS publication in the companion research doc.

- Blighted lot drag: 4 to 20 percent on adjacent properties.
- Greening reversal: up to 17 percent uplift on adjacent properties post-intervention.
- Penn RCT (Branas et al.): 4.3% year-one and 13% six-year cumulative uplift on homes within 1,000 ft of greened lots.
- Penn RCT crime effects: -9.2% all crime, -13.7% burglary at 18 months.
- Donovan & Butry (Portland): single street tree = +$7,130 to fronting home, +$12,828 total including 100-ft spillover.
- Wolf / Hedonic synthesis: +3% to +15% on home value from mature trees.
- Kovacs national meta-analysis (2022): canopy elasticity 4x larger in neighborhoods already above 25% canopy than at 0-10%.
- Voicu & Been: up to +9.4% on surrounding properties from a community garden, largest in poorest neighborhoods.
- i-Tree per-tree value: ~$3,918 over 30 years per mature tree, including ~$1,560 in stormwater alone.
- Arbor Day / USFS ROI: $2 to $5 returned per $1 spent on urban tree care.
- Energy: 30% AC reduction, 20-50% heating reduction with properly placed trees.
